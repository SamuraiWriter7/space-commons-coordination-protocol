# Space Debris Responsibility Record v0.5

## Purpose

Space Debris Responsibility Record defines the minimum record structure for documenting debris-related events, uncertainty, mitigation actions, remediation contributions, and public accountability in space safety coordination.

Its purpose is not to create a blame engine.

Its purpose is to preserve a traceable and reviewable record of debris-related responsibility without converting uncertainty into hostility.

This layer connects:

* v0.1 Space Friendship Event
* v0.2 Neutral Coordination Node
* v0.3 De-escalation Workflow
* v0.4 AI Misclassification Audit
* debris mitigation
* responsibility review
* remediation contribution

The core question is:

What debris-related event occurred, what is known, what remains uncertain, and what mitigation or remediation actions were taken?

## Why This Layer Matters

Space debris is a shared-risk problem.

A single debris event may affect multiple operators, orbital regions, and future missions.

However, attribution can be uncertain.

A debris object may be untracked.
Its origin may be disputed.
The observation may be incomplete.
AI may over-classify the risk.
Public interpretation may become escalatory.

SCCP v0.5 provides a structured way to record debris-related responsibility while preserving uncertainty, human review, and de-escalation boundaries.

## Record Scope

A Space Debris Responsibility Record may document:

* debris event identity
* linked Space Friendship Event
* affected objects or orbital regions
* debris origin assessment
* uncertainty level
* responsibility status
* mitigation actions
* remediation contributions
* public disclosure status
* human review
* recurrence prevention

## Responsibility Boundary

This record does not automatically assign legal liability.

It may record responsibility status such as:

* unknown
* under review
* disputed
* not attributable
* confirmed by human review

No final attribution should be made by AI.

AI may assist with object correlation, trajectory uncertainty estimation, risk classification, and summary drafting.

AI must not finalize responsibility or hostile attribution.

## Mitigation and Remediation

The protocol distinguishes between:

### Mitigation

Actions taken to reduce immediate risk.

Examples:

* notify affected operator
* update conjunction warning
* adjust monitoring frequency
* perform avoidance planning
* publish non-sensitive debris warning

### Remediation

Actions taken to repair, compensate, reduce recurrence, or contribute to shared orbital safety.

Examples:

* contribute observation data
* support debris tracking
* fund remediation study
* update operational practice
* share lessons learned
* improve model or validator
* participate in coordinated cleanup planning

## Public Disclosure

Debris records may contain sensitive operational data.

Therefore, public disclosure should be tiered.

The protocol supports:

* public non-sensitive summary
* operator-only access
* tiered access
* restricted review

The default should be transparency without exposing sensitive operational details.

## Design Boundary

Space Debris Responsibility Record is not:

* a punishment system
* an automatic liability engine
* a hostile attribution mechanism
* a replacement for international law
* a trigger for retaliation

It is:

* a traceability record
* a responsibility review structure
* a mitigation record
* a remediation contribution log
* a recurrence prevention layer
* a commons-preserving safety mechanism

## Core Statement

Before blame, trace.

Before attribution, review.

Before punishment, mitigation.

Before escalation, remediation.

Before sovereignty, orbital commons.
